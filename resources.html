1 Exit Readiness Framework

Before listing your business, ensure you're prepared for a successful sale. This framework helps you assess and improve your exit readiness.

Financial Clean-Up

3 years of clean financials, clear SDE calculation, separate personal expenses

Documentation

SOPs, employee handbook, vendor contracts, customer agreements organized

Owner Dependency

Reduce reliance on owner, document key processes, cross-train staff

Legal & Compliance

Licenses current, no pending litigation, IP protected, contracts assignable

2 Seller's Timeline Checklist

A typical business sale takes 6-12 months. Here's what to expect at each stage.

  • Months 1-2: Valuation, exit prep, marketing materials created
  • Months 2-4: Confidential marketing, buyer inquiries, NDA signing
  • Months 4-6: Buyer meetings, site visits, offers received
  • Months 6-8: Letter of Intent (LOI) signed, due diligence begins
  • Months 8-10: Due diligence completion, purchase agreement drafted
  • Months 10-12: Financing finalized, closing, transition period

2 Due Diligence Checklist

Critical areas to investigate before closing on any acquisition.

  • Financial: Tax returns (3-5 years), bank statements, A/R aging, debt schedule
  • Legal: Corporate documents, contracts, litigation history, IP ownership
  • Operations: Equipment condition, inventory valuation, lease terms, key vendors
  • Customers: Concentration analysis, churn rates, contract terms, satisfaction
  • Employees: Org chart, compensation, key person risk, benefits obligations
  • Regulatory: Licenses, permits, compliance history, environmental issues

1 Common Valuation Methods

The three primary approaches used to value small and mid-size businesses.

Income Approach (SDE/EBITDA Multiple)

Most common for SMBs. Value = Earnings × Industry Multiple. Typical SDE multiples: 2-4x

Market Approach (Comparables)

Based on recent sales of similar businesses. Requires good market data.

Asset Approach

Value of tangible + intangible assets minus liabilities. Floor for valuation.

Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

Present value of future cash flows. Used for larger deals or high-growth businesses.

2 Understanding SDE vs EBITDA

Two key earnings metrics used in business valuation.

  • SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings): Net Income + Owner Salary + Owner Benefits + Interest + Depreciation + Amortization + One-Time/Non-Recurring Expenses
  • EBITDA: Net Income + Interest + Taxes + Depreciation + Amortization (does NOT add back owner salary)
  • When to use SDE: Owner-operated businesses under $5M in value
  • When to use EBITDA: Larger businesses with professional management
  • Key difference: SDE assumes new owner will work in the business; EBITDA assumes hired management

2 Process Improvement Methodology

A simplified Lean approach for small business operations.

  • Map Current State: Document how work actually flows today (not how it "should")
  • Identify Waste: Look for waiting, rework, overprocessing, motion, inventory
  • Design Future State: Eliminate non-value-add steps, reduce handoffs
  • Implement Changes: Start small, measure results, iterate
  • Standardize: Document the new process, train the team, monitor compliance
